What makes a great producer

...and how to fake it

Interactive producers come from so many different backgrounds, and I love that these different backgrounds contribute to them having their own style in how they work with teams and clients. But no matter what background they come from, there are four core traits that all successful producers have. If they are naturally good at these things, or work hard to fake them, they go a long way.

Flexible

They don’t stick to a plan — they manage it, expertly changing course as things inevitably come up during a project. They do this with the needs of all stakeholders in mind, who are none the wiser that the course is being constantly corrected. How to fake it: If you’re not naturally flexible, focus only on the things you know you can’t change, whether that be launch date or budget or key dependencies. Remind yourself that the other things don’t matter and there is always a possible solution. Brainstorm with your project leads or colleagues for alternate solutions before insisting on a plan that hasn’t adapted to the circumstances.

Calm

In the face of problems, they bring a sane and calming influence to the team instead of escalating stress. They are problem-solvers, not red-flag raisers. How to fake it: If you can’t keep your stress on the inside, let it out when you’re not in the office. Never go to your project teams or management with a problem, always approach them with possible solutions for discussion.

Strategic

They interrogate briefs, understand their client’s needs, guide ideas that will meet these needs, and are able to keep a check on the overarching goals throughout a project. How to fake it: Insist on a project brief and, if your client doesn’t give you one, create one. For every client ask or solution posed, refer back to the goals and success criteria to make sure the suggestions are smart and on brief, speak up if they’re not.

Meticulous

They have a microscopic attention to detail. No-one else on the team is in a position to make sure everything is thought of at the beginning of a project, and that everything is correct throughout. How to fake it: Make checklists for yourself, give yourself extra time to check everything that needs to be checked, and always ask for a second pair of eyes when something is critical.
